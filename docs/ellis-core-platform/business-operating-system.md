# Phase E — Business Operating System

| Capability | Repeatable lifecycle |
| --- | --- |
| Service packages and pricing | Operations owns versioned services, packages, scope, pricing, and eligibility. |
| Sales and discovery | Sales CRM progresses Prospect → Discovery → Proposal → Client; contacts and conversations remain attached to the canonical business. |
| Proposal workflow | Approved discovery produces a versioned proposal; acceptance creates the agreement and delivery project. |
| Onboarding and delivery | A reusable onboarding checklist launches projects/tasks, approvals, deliverables, and outcome measures. |
| Offboarding | Transfer access, apply retention, review outcomes, and schedule follow-up. |
| Testimonials and case studies | Record consent; approved outcomes become case studies and optional portfolio items. |
| Referrals and portfolio | Track referral source/outcome; portfolio is an approved projection of demos/case studies. |
| Growth Engine | Operates the documented monitor-to-learning lifecycle below. |

## Growth Engine — first internal product

The Growth Engine acquires clients while people focus on sales, discovery, delivery, and relationships. It is composed from Core Platform domains, not a separate monolith:

`Monitor Ellis → Website/Business Intelligence → Strategy/Campaign → Copy/Creative → QA + human approval → Publishing → Analytics → Learning → next strategy`.

It uses the same canonical Brand Profiles, Services, Campaigns, Content Assets, Publishing Jobs, and Analytics records as client work. Measure qualified pipeline, campaign conversion, approved-plan-to-publication time, publishing success, human review time, cost per qualified lead, and reusable assets per campaign.
